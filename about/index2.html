ABOUT US

WHO WE ARE

Forest Focus is a forestry dedicated organization aiming at reconstruction and reformation of different forest levels from agroforests and forest gardens to Natural forests. We are driven by a philosophy that for humanity to excel forests of different sorts should well be managed and protected. There is no doubt that natural resources have a direct knot to individual lives in all communities however tempered forestry ecosystems no matter the scope have been a key ignition to various Global social economic problems. To make the world a better place Forest focus seeks to dictate planting of multipurpose environmentally friendly trees with quick economic and ecological impact to the communities. This refabricates an old mode which at most points included trees with higher allelopathic characteristics or intensively degraded soils in local regions. To succeed this Forest focus starts with traditional soils revitalization methods which are proven effective, cheaper and easily accessible by village peasants to expand village productive land. Increasing available productive land by fortifying the isolated degraded portions will somehow press down the population need for land against which is available. A percentage cost from each tree planted is allocated to community development projects such as funding education for young girls and disabled country side residents as to minimize the impact of extreme poverty.

VISION

To have communities with healthy environmental surroundings for sustainable development.

MISSION

To mobilize and empower the local community in seizing available untapped opportunities, maintaining healthy environment and promoting environmental related rights.

PARTNERSHIP

The Forest focus works with worldwide tree stake holders to tiptoe to its mission. Currently two sister projects are at primary stages in Muheza, Mkinga and Handeni the Tanga region districts frequently hit by hunger in rural communities due to rainfall overdependent agriculture. In these localities Forest Focus engages peasants to utilize any available moisture to grow and produce resilient crops and producing trees. Understanding how soils are important Forest focus helps soils revitalization activities in both projects. In the time being our project targets to plant trees which should at least prove to provide three direct and quick economic impact such as food (fruits, nuts, roots, fodder), Soil revitalization (Nitrogen fixation, source of organic carbon) and wood (Multiple branching trees). When this gets achieved hunger and its associate poverty and environmental destruction in rural communities will be driven away.
